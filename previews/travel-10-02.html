Part 1: Why Asia

1. What Western Media Doesn't Tell You

The News Only Shows 1% of Asia

What does Asia look like in Western news? Typhoons, tsunamis, earthquakes. Terrorism, conflict, dictatorships. Poverty, disease, chaos. That's the Asia Westerners see on TV.

But this is less than 1% of Asia. News reports incidents. Daily life isn't news. Millions of people peacefully commuting in Bangkok isn't news. Lost items being returned 100% of the time in Tokyo isn't news.

I've traveled to 100+ countries. Europe, the Americas, Africa, Oceania—I've been to all of them. Objectively speaking, Asia is the safest continent I've ever traveled.

European pickpockets, South American muggers, African scammers. These problems are significantly less common in Asia. Of course, crime exists in Asia too. But crime targeting tourists is far rarer than in Europe.

What Western Media Doesn't Tell You
  • Japan's crime rate is among the world's lowest. Lose your wallet and it comes back.
  • Singapore is a city-state so clean it even regulates chewing gum.
  • Thailand welcomes 40 million tourists annually.
  • Vietnam welcomes American tourists. The war ended 50 years ago.

2. Five Ways Asia Beats Europe

Travel Twice as Much for the Same Money

First, cost. Average daily expense in Europe: €100-150. In Asia: $30-50. For the same two-week trip, Europe costs $3,000, Asia costs $1,000.

Second, food. A €15-30 meal at a European restaurant costs $2-5 in Asia. And that's Michelin Guide-recognized street food.

Third, diversity. Europe feels samey. Cathedrals, plazas, cafes. Asia is completely different from country to country. Japanese minimalism, Indian chaos, Thai smiles, Chinese scale.

From a 100+-Country Traveler
I've been pickpocketed in Paris. Attempted in Barcelona, too, and Rome. But in 30 Asian countries—not once.
